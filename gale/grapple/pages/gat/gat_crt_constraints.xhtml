The Constraints Tab of the Editor

The Constraints Tab of the PRT Editor is shown below:

The constraints tab is intended to define three types of constraints on how the PRT can be used in the tool: You can specify whether it is allowed to have a cycle of PRT instances (of the same PRT) or not. For instance, a cycle of prerequisites is undesirable as none of the concepts would ever become suitable, and a cycle of "knowledge propagation" would generate an infinite loop. You can specify that only concepts with a certain (default) value for some attribute may be inserted into one of the sockets. You may also disallow that two concepts must not also appear in a socket of a different PRT that makes use of a common variable. Currently the CAM tool ignores all constraint specifications so they have no effect and are only included to allow the creator of the PRT to express desired constraints.
