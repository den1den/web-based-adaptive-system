The GALE IfModule (<if> tag)

A fragment can be included in the source (XML or XHTML) text of a resource (page), and only conditionally presented to the user. This can be done using the <gale:if>, <gale:then> and <gale:else> tags with a GALE expression (added to the <gale:if> tag) to make the decision on the inclusion. This construct is simple and most useful for conditional inclusions that occur in a single instance. An example: 

<gale:if expr="${#suitability}">
  <gale:then>
    This concept is suitable :) 
  </gale:then>
  <gale:else>
    This concept is unsuitable!
  </gale:else>
</gale:if>
The example expression evaluates the "suitability" of the current concept and then replaces the entire if-then-else DOM subtree by the contents of the "then" or the "else" part. (Here that is just a text fragment but it can also be a large part of the document with many tags inside. In any case the "then" and "else" parts must be valid XML fragments.)

This tutorial contains <if> tags in a number of places, typically to include a small explanation of a topic you have not yet studied. Such conditionally included fragments typically depend either on the number of visits to a concept or on the knowledge of a concept.
